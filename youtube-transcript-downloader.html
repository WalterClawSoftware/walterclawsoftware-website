YouTube transcript downloader

A desktop way to save transcripts from captioned YouTube videos.

ClipScript runs on Mac and Windows. Paste a YouTube URL or video ID, fetch the available caption transcript, keep it in a local archive, and export practical transcript files when you need them.

It is intentionally narrower than a transcription service. ClipScript works with videos that already have accessible captions or transcript data; it does not create new transcripts for videos with no captions.

What it does

Download the transcript, not the whole video workflow.

This page is for the specific search intent: getting transcript text out of YouTube videos that already expose captions.

Fetch caption transcripts

Paste the YouTube URL or video ID. ClipScript requests transcript data for that specific video and shows you when captions are unavailable.

Keep a local archive

Saved transcript history stays on your computer so you can reopen, search, favorite, and organize transcript text without rebuilding the same browser search.

Export useful files

FAQ

Short answers before you try ClipScript.

These answers mirror the current ClipScript product and privacy copy.

Does ClipScript require Google sign-in or a YouTube API key?

No. The current workflow does not require Google OAuth, a separate Google sign-in, a YouTube API key, or a connected channel session.

Does ClipScript transcribe videos that do not have captions?

No. ClipScript is for YouTube videos that already have accessible captions or transcript data. It does not invent a transcript for videos with no captions.

What formats can ClipScript export?

Current product copy lists TXT, SRT, VTT, and JSON exports.
