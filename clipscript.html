Workflow

From captioned YouTube video to reusable working copy.

The practical flow is simple: paste the video, fetch and search the transcript, then save or export the material into the next thing you are making.

1. Paste the YouTube URL or video ID

Start with the exact video you want to work from. ClipScript accepts common YouTube watch URLs, share URLs, Shorts URLs, and raw video IDs.

2. Fetch, read, and search

Fetch the available caption track, review it with timestamps or grouped intervals, search inside it, and use Open at Time when you need source context.

3. Save, favorite, and export

Keep transcripts in a local archive, mark important entries as favorites, and export the formats that fit your writing, editing, caption, or research workflow.

Local archive and favorites

Reopen useful transcripts instead of fetching the same material again or losing it in scattered files.

Timestamp-aware reading

Read with timestamps, hide them for cleaner copy, group text into intervals, or jump back to the source video when context matters.

Multi-format export

Copy, save, and export as TXT, SRT, VTT, or JSON, or export a full archive folder with a manifest file.

Who is ClipScript for?

ClipScript is for creators, editors, assistants, researchers, and other people who need transcripts from captioned YouTube videos and want a faster desktop workflow.

Do I need a Google account, API key, or channel connection?

No. ClipScript's current workflow does not require Google sign-in, Google OAuth, a YouTube API key, or a connected channel session. You paste a YouTube URL or video ID and fetch the transcript directly.

ClipScript tells you clearly when no captions are available for that video. It works with videos that already have manual captions or auto-generated captions available.
