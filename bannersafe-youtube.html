Crop proof

The site uses the same test banner that exercised the app prototype. The important signal is not decoration; it is whether a logo, mascot, subtitle, or other protected content remains inside YouTube's effective crop zones.

Protected-region safety

Users mark critical parts of the existing art, then the app validates whether those regions survive the crop guides.

Placement modes with a purpose

Max Impact favors larger artwork, Balanced uses moderate margins, and Safest adds extra protection for crop-sensitive content.

Local-first desktop workflow

The core job is handled locally: import an image, preview crop safety, adjust placement, and export the final upload image.

Workflow

A focused pass between banner design and YouTube upload.

The best user already has banner art. BannerSafe helps them stop fighting YouTube's changing preview crops by making the safe regions visible before export.

1. Import existing art

Open a PNG or JPG that already represents the channel banner artwork.

2. Mark protected regions

Draw boxes around the logo, mascot, face, subtitle, or other content that must survive.

3. Preview and fit

Use the canvas, crop guides, scale, position, and placement modes to find a safer composition.

4. Export for upload

Save the final 2560 x 1440 image for YouTube channel banner upload.
